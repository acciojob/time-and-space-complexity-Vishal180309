Understanding Time and Space Complexity

Time complexity refers to the amount of time an algorithm takes to complete, usually measured in terms of the number of operations performed. An efficient algorithm has a low time complexity, allowing it to solve problems quickly.

Space complexity, on the other hand, refers to the amount of memory an algorithm uses. Space Complexity is often measured in terms of the maximum amount of memory used, denoted as O(n), where n is the size of the input. A good algorithm should strive to minimize both time and space complexity.
